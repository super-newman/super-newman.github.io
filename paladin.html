Paladin

Aggro Paladin

Strategy

  • Establish board control quickly using sticky minions and weapons
  • Keep up momentum using Divine Favor
  • Use minion buffs for small damage bursts (or with divine shields for efficient trades)

Common Counter Strategies

  • Break divine shields before minions can be buffed
  • Empty your hand so they can’t get as much benefit from Divine Favor

Midrange Paladin

Strategy

  • Slow the opponent’s start with cards like Zombie Chow, Coghammer, and Consecration
  • Extend the game using taunts, Antique Healbot, and Lay on Hands
  • Overwhelm the opponent with big minions and Quartermaster-buffed Silver Hand Recruits

Common Counter Strategies

  • Clear Silver Hand Recruits to avoid Quartermaster buffs
  • Avoid overextending into Equality and/or Consecration
  • Save silence for Tirion Fording if possible

Murloc Paladin

Strategy

  • Extend the game with taunts, healing, and extensive removal
  • Play murlocs throughout the game
  • Finish the opponent with Anyfin Can Happen in the late game

Common Counter Strategies

  • Play around board clears
  • Keep the board clear to deny them cheap Solemn Vigils
  • Remove key murlocs (Old Murk-Eye, Murloc Warleader) without killing them (Entomb, Hex, Polymorph, etc.)
  • Keep health high and/or taunts up to stay out of Anyfin Can Happen lethal range

Secret Paladin

Strategy

  • Establish an early board presence using sticky minions and secrets
  • Play Mysterious Challenger on turn 6 to extend board advantage to an overwhelming margin

Common Counter Strategies

  • Become adept at playing around paladin secrets
  • Try to shut down their board heading into Turn 6
  • Practice responding effectively to Turn 6
    • Spend time during turn 5 planning out your response to a turn 6 Mysterious Challenger, so you can begin the turn 6 play (and long-lasting secret resolution animations) quickly
    • Keep removal on hand -- for example, Big Game Hunter can take down an Avenged Mysterious Challenger
    • Removing a minion with a spell first can help direct the effects of Avenge, since attacking first will likely summon a new minion via Noble Sacrifice
